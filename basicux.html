What i've learned

With a design sprint you have to follow certain steps, and that experience was both very fun and hard.
Limiting yourself to an amount of time to come up with creative things while still remaining open to sometimes crazy ideas, was what I found most challenging about this project.

The user testing part was very fun to execute, but also quite difficult. Asking the right questions might seem like a breeze, but it’s actually a very tough task.


What i'd change

I would probably change the design and color scheme of this prototype…

What i've learned

As earlier mentioned, I was far off happy with the visual part of our prototype. I tried to start over but hit a bunch of challenges that could’ve been limited by going with what my group and I had already created.

With this I was taught a valuable lesson! In real life, I could easily find myself in a situation, where a costumer has a clear vision that I don’t visually agree with at all… and I should be able to deliver a high-quality piece of work regardless!

Lesson learned!


What i'd change

I would redo my remake to match the prototype we made in the design sprint, to strengthen my ability to work with design that I might not agree with.
